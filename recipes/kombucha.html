Kombucha

Yields 5-6 17oz bottles

Ingredients

Tea 1 gallon water
1 cup sugar
8 black tea bags
Flavorings
Dealers choice, a couple strips of ginger and a some blueberries is a favorite

Directions


Prepare new tea
Make new tea with 1 gallon water, 8 black tea bags, 1 cup sugar. Exact method doesn’t really matter. Let cool.
Bottle fermented tea
Put flavoring into 5-6 tall skinny 17 oz fermentation bottles (vary size/number as you like but you want to fill it up to a thin neck and a stopper to ensure carbonation; less surface area exposed to air is better).
Take mother out of previous batch in gallon jug and set aside. Reserve 2 cups of the fermented tea. Decant the rest of the fermented tea into the prepared bottles.
Set capped kombucha bottles aside at room temp for a day or two, depending on your preference of carbonation (and accounting for summer/winter room temp). Consider storing on a baking sheet in case carbonation forces leakage.
Make sure not to over-carbonate, especially during warmer months, to ensure bottles don’t break and burst. Move to fridge to halt carbonation.
Bottle new tea
Put reserved 2 cups fermented tea back in gallon jug, no need to soap, along with cooled new tea and mother. The reserved fermented tea is to ensure new tea acidity starts high enough to prevent bad bacteria.
Cover with a kitchen towel and a rubber band so mother can breathe but bugs can’t get in.
Set gallon jug aside at room temp for a week or so, depending on your preference of fermentation (and accounting for summer/winter room temp). The longer it ferments with the mother the less sweet and the more vinegary it will become.
This is now the gallon jug you’ll use back at the beginning of the recipe. To drink, optionally filter kombucha through mesh screen to catch flavorings and accumulated goop, serve cold, and enjoy!
